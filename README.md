Ab Initio Structure Prediction of Ferritin (1FHA)
Author: Sude Birsen SAMANCI

Institution: Inonu University - Department of Molecular Biology and Genetics

Course: Molecular Modeling

Project Overview
In this project, the 3D structure of Ferritin (PDB ID: 1FHA), a critical protein responsible for intracellular iron storage and the prevention of cellular toxicity, was modeled ab initio. Ferritin plays a vital role in cellular homeostasis by binding free iron (Fe²⁺), oxidizing it to (Fe³⁺), and sequestering it within an inorganic mineral core. This physical encapsulation prevents free iron from reacting with hydrogen peroxide (the Fenton reaction), thereby protecting the cell's DNA, proteins, and lipids from destructive hydroxyl radicals and severe oxidative stress.

Methodology
The structural modeling was performed using AlphaFold.

The target sequence (derived from the 1FHA complex, Chain A) was utilized to generate 5 different structural conformations.

Among the generated conformations, model_0 was selected as the reference structure due to its highest local confidence ranking for the target chain (pLDDT: 95.61).

To assess structural accuracy, a sequence-independent alignment was performed in PyMOL. Following a rigorous 5-cycle outlier rejection protocol (rejecting 104, 44, 19, 5, and 2 atoms successively), the structural superimposition yielded an exceptional RMSD of 0.336 Å (across 1164 aligned atoms). This exceptionally low RMSD confirms the high conformational stability and structural reliability of the selected model.

Structural Analysis and Visualization
The predicted .cif model was imported into PyMOL and colored according to the per-residue confidence score (pLDDT) metrics to evaluate regional stability:

Blue (pLDDT > 90): High accuracy expected (Highly structured core domains, characteristic of the alpha-helical bundle).

Cyan/Yellow (90 > pLDDT > 50): Moderate confidence (Flexible loop regions connecting the helices).

Orange/Red (pLDDT < 50): Intrinsically disordered or highly flexible terminal tail regions.

Highlighted Structural Features: In the high-resolution PyMOL render (ferritin_pymol_render.png), the monomeric chain is specifically isolated. The visualization highlights its characteristic four-helix bundle motif, which structurally acts as the fundamental building block for assembling the massive 24-subunit iron-storage nanocage.
